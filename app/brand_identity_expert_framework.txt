=== EXPERT BRAND IDENTITY EVALUATION FRAMEWORK (Marc Gallo, Senior Designer) ===

HOW TO EVALUATE — SEQUENCE (80% intuition, 20% analysis):

STEP 1 — FIRST IMPRESSION (Taste Level)
Does the work feel considered or noisy?
Is there a clear idea driving the visual decisions?
Does it show discipline — restraint, not decoration?

STEP 2 — QUALITY OF THINKING
What was the problem this brand needed to solve?
What is the strategy behind the identity?
Why THIS solution and not another?

STEP 3 — VISUAL SYSTEM
Are structures and assets coherent?
Can it scale — across campaigns, across designers?
Is there distinctiveness — does it stand apart?
Is there consistency of intelligence — same standards applied throughout?
Depth of decision-making visible in:
  - Typography: facing, language, architecture
  - How constraints were handled
  - Clarity and distinctness of the mark

STEP 4 — INDUSTRY READINESS
Is the designer technically capable?
Are they strategically interesting — not just executers?
Case studies should show: logo first then system thinking
Mockups should support the idea, not replace it

SCORE 4-5 SIGNALS:
- Idea is the root, not just execution or taste
- Sharp understanding of what the project means
- Structural integrity — specific decisions under pressure
- Work feels inevitable, not just obvious
- Evidence of decision-making at every level
- Knows when to push and when to restrain

SCORE 1-2 RED FLAGS:
- Polish without a clear point of view
- Sleek but shallow — looks good, means little
- No explanation of what changed or what the brand gained
- Generic brand language — could belong to any brand
- No evidence of decision-making — just output
- Mockups used for selling, not supporting the idea
- Typography decorative, not part of brand language

CRITICAL RULE:
The question is not does it look good?
but does it think well?
Polish without point of view = not hire-worthy.

=== END MARC GALLO FRAMEWORK ===
